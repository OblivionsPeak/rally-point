Your Three Appeal Options

Supplemental Claim
Best if: new evidence

Submit new and relevant evidence the VA didn't have before — a nexus letter from your doctor, a buddy statement, new medical records, or a private medical opinion. The VA must consider it.

Timeline: 4–5 months average
Preserves effective date: Yes, if filed within 1 year
Best for: You have a doctor willing to write a nexus letter, or you have records the VA missed
Higher-Level Review (HLR)
Best if: VA made an error

A senior VA rater reviews your existing file with fresh eyes. No new evidence allowed — this is for when you believe the VA misapplied the law, ignored existing evidence, or made a clear error.

Timeline: 4–5 months average
Preserves effective date: Yes, if filed within 1 year
Best for: The rating decision ignored evidence already in your file, or the examiner's report was clearly wrong
Board Appeal (BVA)
Best if: complex case

Appeal to the Board of Veterans Appeals. A Veterans Law Judge reviews your case. Slowest path but most thorough — and the board can order new C&P exams or development on your behalf.

Timeline: 1–3 years depending on lane chosen
Preserves effective date: Yes, if filed within 1 year
Best for: Complex cases, long history of denials, or you want a hearing before a judge
Get free help: A VSO (Veterans Service Officer) from the DAV, VFW, American Legion, or your state veterans affairs office will review your case and help you choose the right path — at no cost. Find one at va.gov/decision-reviews/get-help-with-review-request
