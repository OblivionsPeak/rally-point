Tips for a strong buddy statement
  • Be specific. Include dates, locations, unit names, and exact events where possible.
  • First-hand only. The author should only describe what they personally saw or experienced — not what someone else told them.
  • Connect to the condition. Explicitly link the witnessed event or behavior to the claimed disability.
  • Describe current impact. VA raters want to understand how the condition affects daily life, work, and relationships today.
  • Keep it factual. Avoid emotional language or exaggeration — credibility matters more than sympathy.
  • Attach to VA Form 21-4138. The author should complete an official VA Form 21-4138 (Personal Statement in Support of Claim) and attach this document as a continuation sheet.
