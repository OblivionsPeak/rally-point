DBQ Reference Guide

Find the right Disability Benefits Questionnaire for your condition and bring it to your doctor appointment.

What is a DBQ?
A VA form that structures a medical opinion exactly the way raters read it. VA raters use DBQs to assign ratings — a private doctor filling one out carries the same weight as a C&P exam.
Why use a private DBQ?
VA C&P examiners are often rushed and incentivized to minimize. Your own treating physician knows your worst days. A private DBQ submitted as evidence can result in a higher rating than the VA's own exam.
How to use this guide
Find your condition below, note the DBQ name, then search for it on VA.gov or ask your VSO to print it. Bring the blank form to your doctor and ask them to complete it.
Range of motion is critical
For musculoskeletal conditions, the doctor must measure and record range of motion. Emphasize your worst days — the VA rates based on the worst presentation, not average.

How to get blank DBQ forms
  1. Search va.gov/disability/how-to-file-claim/forms for the DBQ name above, or ask your VSO — they have them all.
  2. Print the blank form and bring it to your doctor appointment.
  3. Ask your doctor to complete it based on your current diagnosis and examination findings.
  4. Make sure the doctor signs and dates the form and includes their credentials and contact info.
  5. Submit the completed DBQ as evidence with your VA claim or as a supplemental document to an appeal.
Tip: If you're preparing for a VA C&P exam, having a private DBQ already completed by your treating physician gives the rater a second, often more favorable, medical opinion they must consider.
