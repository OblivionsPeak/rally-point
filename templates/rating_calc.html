100% — Check P&T Eligibility

At 100% combined, you may qualify for Permanent & Total (P&T) status, which locks in your rating and opens additional benefits: Chapter 35 DEA education for dependents, property tax exemptions (varies by state), CHAMPVA healthcare for dependents, and more. Ask your VSO about applying for P&T if you're not already designated.

Consider TDIU (Individual Unemployability)

If your disabilities prevent you from holding substantially gainful employment, you may qualify for Total Disability Individual Unemployability (TDIU) — which pays at the 100% rate even if your combined rating is lower. Basic threshold: one disability at 60%+ OR combined rating of 70%+ with at least one disability at 40%+.

Why VA Math Works This Way
The Whole Person Method The VA treats you as a "whole person" (100%). Your highest disability takes a percentage of that whole person. Each subsequent disability takes its percentage of what's left — not of 100%.
Rounding to Nearest 10% After combining, the VA rounds to the nearest 10%. Values ending in 1–4 round down; 5–9 round up. So a combined value of 85% rounds to 90%, while 84% rounds to 80%.
Order Matters Ratings are always applied highest-to-lowest. This maximizes the benefit — applying a smaller rating first would leave less "whole person" for the larger one to work against.
Bilateral Factor If you have disabilities affecting both arms or both legs (bilateral), a 10% bonus is added before the final combination. This calculator does not include the bilateral factor — note it if it applies to you.
