BURN PITS & AIRBORNE HAZARDS

The PACT Act created presumptive service connection for veterans exposed to burn pits, open air disposal, and other airborne hazards in Southwest Asia and other locations.

Presumptive Cancers & Conditions
  • Head or neck cancer (any type)
  • Respiratory system cancer (lung, bronchus, larynx, trachea)
  • Gastrointestinal cancer (esophagus, stomach, colon, rectum, anus, liver, gall bladder, bile duct, pancreas)
  • Reproductive system cancer
  • Urinary tract cancer (kidney, bladder, ureter, urethra)
  • Lymphatic cancer (Hodgkin's, non-Hodgkin's lymphoma)
  • Melanoma
  • Any rare cancer (affects fewer than 200,000 Americans per year)
  • Glioblastoma
  • Squamous cell carcinoma of the head or neck
  • Constrictive bronchiolitis or obliterative bronchiolitis
  • Constrictive pericarditis
  • Any illness VA determines is associated with particulate matter exposure
Next step: File a claim for any qualifying cancer or respiratory condition using VA Form 21-526EZ. Your DD-214 showing qualifying service location and dates is your primary evidence — no nexus letter required. Denied before Aug 2022? File a Supplemental Claim — PACT Act applies retroactively.
AGENT ORANGE — VIETNAM

Veterans who served in Vietnam or its territorial waters are presumed to have been exposed to Agent Orange. No proof of actual exposure needed.

Presumptive Conditions
  • AL amyloidosis
  • Bladder cancer
  • Chronic B-cell leukemia
  • Chloracne (or similar acneform disease)
  • Diabetes mellitus type 2
  • Hodgkin's disease
  • Ischemic heart disease
  • Multiple myeloma
  • Non-Hodgkin's lymphoma
  • Parkinson's disease
  • Peripheral neuropathy (early-onset)
  • Porphyria cutanea tarda
  • Prostate cancer
  • Respiratory cancers (lung, larynx, trachea, bronchus)
  • Soft-tissue sarcomas (many types)
  • Hypertension (added by PACT Act 2022)
  • Monoclonal gammopathy of undetermined significance (MGUS) — added by PACT Act
Next step: File for any condition on this list. Show Vietnam service via DD-214. If previously denied for a condition now on this list, file a Supplemental Claim. Hypertension was just added — if you have it and served in Vietnam, file now.
AGENT ORANGE — BLUE WATER NAVY

The PACT Act extended Agent Orange presumptive status to Blue Water Navy veterans who served offshore Vietnam — reversing decades of VA denials. Same presumptive conditions as Vietnam in-country service apply.

Qualifying Ships

If your ship is on the VA's Blue Water Navy ship list, you qualify automatically. If it's not, you may still qualify by showing the ship entered Vietnam's inland waterways or docked in Vietnam.

Next step: Check the VA's ship list at va.gov. File for any Agent Orange presumptive condition. If denied previously as Blue Water Navy, this is grounds for a Supplemental Claim — the law changed in your favor.
AGENT ORANGE — KOREAN DMZ

Agent Orange and other herbicides were used along the Korean DMZ. Veterans who served there during this window have presumptive exposure. Same full list of Agent Orange presumptive conditions applies.

Next step: File for any Agent Orange presumptive condition. Your DD-214 must show service in Korea during the qualifying dates. A buddy statement confirming DMZ duty area can strengthen the claim.
AGENT ORANGE — C-123 AIRCRAFT

C-123 aircraft used to spray Agent Orange remained contaminated for years. Veterans who flew on or maintained these aircraft after the Vietnam War are now presumed to have been exposed.

Next step: File for any Agent Orange presumptive condition. You'll need service records showing your unit, aircraft tail numbers, and dates of service with those aircraft. If records are unavailable, request them from the National Personnel Records Center.
CAMP LEJEUNE WATER CONTAMINATION

The water supply at Camp Lejeune was contaminated with volatile organic compounds (TCE, PCE, benzene, vinyl chloride) for over 30 years. This includes family members who lived on base.

Presumptive Conditions
  • Bladder cancer
  • Kidney cancer
  • Liver cancer
  • Non-Hodgkin's lymphoma
  • Adult leukemia
  • Multiple myeloma
  • Parkinson's disease
  • Neurobehavioral effects
  • Renal toxicity
  • Female infertility
  • Scleroderma
  • Miscarriage
  • Hepatic steatosis
Next step: File a VA disability claim AND consider a Camp Lejeune Justice Act lawsuit (separate civil claim). Veterans, family members, and civilians who were exposed for 30+ days may be eligible. Use housing records, orders, or base rosters to document your time there.
IONIZING RADIATION EXPOSURE

Veterans who participated in nuclear weapons testing, occupied Hiroshima or Nagasaki after WWII, or were POWs in Japan may have been exposed to ionizing radiation.

Presumptive Cancers
  • All cancers (except chronic lymphocytic leukemia) are presumptive for qualifying radiation veterans
  • Leukemia (other than CLL)
  • Thyroid cancer
  • Breast cancer
  • Lung cancer
  • Bone cancer
  • Liver cancer
  • Skin cancer (melanoma)
  • Esophageal cancer
  • Stomach cancer
  • Colon cancer
  • Urinary tract cancers
  • Salivary gland cancer
  • Multiple myeloma
  • Lymphomas (non-Hodgkin's, Hodgkin's)
Next step: Request a Radiation Dose Assessment from the VA (they estimate your exposure dose). File using VA Form 21-526EZ. The DTRA (Defense Threat Reduction Agency) maintains records of nuclear test participants — request your records there if service records are incomplete.
GULF WAR ILLNESS

Gulf War veterans suffer from a range of medically unexplained chronic symptoms collectively called Gulf War Illness. The VA presumes these are service-connected — no nexus letter, no diagnosed cause required.

Presumptive Conditions
  • Medically unexplained chronic multisymptom illness (chronic fatigue, fibromyalgia, functional gastrointestinal disorders)
  • Chronic fatigue syndrome
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Functional gastrointestinal disorders (IBS, functional dyspepsia)
  • Undiagnosed illnesses with symptoms present to a degree of 10% or more
  • Infectious diseases: brucellosis, campylobacter jejuni, coxiella burnetii, malaria, mycobacterium tuberculosis, nontyphoid salmonella, shigella, visceral leishmaniasis, West Nile virus
Next step: File for any of these conditions. The key requirement is that symptoms began during active duty or within 10 years of service in the Southwest Asia theater, and are present to at least 10% disability level. Symptoms don't need a formal diagnosis — document them with medical records and a personal statement.
Previously Denied? PACT Act Is Retroactive.

If you were denied for a condition that is now presumptive under the PACT Act, you can file a Supplemental Claim (VA Form 20-0995) with "PACT Act" as the new and relevant evidence. The VA is required to re-adjudicate under the new law.

If approved, benefits may be paid back to the date of your original claim or August 10, 2022 — whichever is later. Get a VSO to help file. Find a VSO →
