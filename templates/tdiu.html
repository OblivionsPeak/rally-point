Total Disability Based on Individual Unemployability (TDIU)

TDIU allows veterans to receive 100% disability pay even if their combined rating is below 100%, when service-connected conditions prevent them from maintaining substantially gainful employment.

100%
Pay rate with TDIU
What this means in dollars
A veteran rated 70% receives roughly $1,716/month. With TDIU, that same veteran receives the 100% rate of $3,737/month — a difference of over $2,000/month and $24,000/year.
(2024 rates, single veteran with no dependents)
Quick Eligibility Check

Answer these two questions to see if you may qualify for TDIU.

1. What are your current service-connected ratings?
2. Are you unable to work due to your service-connected conditions?
Schedular TDIU
The standard path. You meet the rating thresholds and are unable to work. VA Form 21-8940 is required.

Criteria:
  • One condition rated 60%+, OR
  • Combined rating 70%+ with at least one condition at 40%+
  • Unable to maintain substantially gainful employment
Extra-Schedular TDIU
For veterans who don't meet the rating thresholds but are still unable to work due to their service-connected conditions.

How it works:
  • VA must refer to the Director of Compensation
  • Harder to obtain — requires strong medical and vocational evidence
  • Always worth pursuing if you're truly unemployable

Evidence That Strengthens a TDIU Claim
  • Nexus letter specifically addressing employability — your doctor stating that your service-connected conditions prevent you from maintaining substantially gainful employment
  • Work history showing progressive decline — job loss, frequent absences, demotions, or accommodations related to your condition
  • Social Security Disability Insurance (SSDI) award — not required but very persuasive
  • Vocational expert opinion — a formal assessment stating you are not competitively employable given your conditions, education, and work history
  • Buddy statements from former supervisors or coworkers — documenting how your conditions affected your work performance
  • Private DBQ showing functional limitations — the functional impairment section of the DBQ should document how your condition prevents sustained work activity
Ready to apply? Work with a VSO first.
A VSO will review your ratings, employment history, and medical evidence before you file — for free. TDIU denials are common; a well-prepared application is critical.
